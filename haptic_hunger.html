Haptic Hunger

For those who have spent a career dealing in abstractions—software, stocks and strategies—there is a visceral, biological need to touch something real. We address this Haptic Hunger by offering the resistance of physical materials: the cold weight of stone, the grit of wet clay and the friction of the loom.

"In an era of instant gratification, we offer the perverse privilege of waiting."

We value the clay pot left to oxidize in a curing pit over the polished object bought today. Here, time is not a resource to be saved; it is the essential ingredient that creates value.
